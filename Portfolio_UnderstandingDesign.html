Understanding the Design Process

Challenge

Develop a protype for an app that tackles an issue in your community. Use the tools in the design process to develop your idea from sketches to an interactive digital prototype.

Problem Statement

Although the University of Michigan is a community of over 44,000, students often isolate themselves into smaller communities, bubbles, that impede them from meeting peers outside their immediate circles â€“ departments, sports teams, classes. How do you facilitate introductions and encounters among students from different disciplines living on a large campus?

Iterative Design Process

High-Level Sketches

Early in the design process I brainstormed eight unique poossible solutions to my problem statement. Ensuring I had a diverse set of ideas.

Storyboard Designs

I developed a storyboard expanding on each of my high-level sketches to show the problem, the user's context, their constraints, and the actions they would have to take for each of the proposed solutions.

Personas

I developed 2 pro personas and 1 anti-persona of users that might use my app imagining their needs, motivations and skills.

Story Maps

With the personas I developed in mind, I created a story map of the flow of tasks and activities my users would engage in when using my app to gain context of what their experiences meeting their goals on the app would be like.

Wireframes

I created low fidelity wireframes showing the flow and interactions I envisioned for the app.

Digital Prototype

I created a digital and interactive prototype of the app using Figma.
